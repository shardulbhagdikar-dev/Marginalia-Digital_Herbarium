About the archive

Why keep a herbarium at all?

Every specimen here follows the same small ritual: collect, press between blotting paper, wait two weeks, then label with the date, the place, and whatever seemed worth remembering about the plant that day. Nothing about the process is fast, which is more or less the point.

A pressed leaf is a kind of sentence — it keeps the shape of a moment long after the moment itself has gone.

The full collection is searchable by name, family, or status. If you've found something worth logging, the field notes page takes new submissions.
